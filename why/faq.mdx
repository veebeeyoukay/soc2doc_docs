Common questions, answered honestly.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What exactly is the Commitment Review?">
    You upload your SOC 2 report; we extract every commitment your organization made
    in it — what was promised, the stated cadence, and what demonstrating it at audit
    time implies — and mirror it back in plain language, organized by business
    function. AI does the extraction; a practitioner verifies it before delivery. It
    arrives by email the same day. It is free.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Is my report safe with you?">
    A mutual NDA is executed before upload is even enabled — you get a copy by email
    with the acceptance timestamp. Your authorization for machine and human analysis
    is logged separately. The report is encrypted in transit and at rest, and access
    is restricted to the assessors working your review. The NDA text is public at
    [soc2doc.ai/nda](https://soc2doc.ai/nda).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will you tell me what's wrong with my program?">
    No. The review mirrors your commitments — it does not judge whether your
    organization lives up to them. The free review does no gap analysis: judging the
    program requires assessing the actual organization (interviews, evidence,
    process), which is a paid engagement, not a document read. We are explicit about
    this so the free review never pretends to be assurance.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who reads my report?">
    The automated extraction pipeline, then a vetted assessor who verifies the output
    before it reaches you. Assessors work under written confidentiality obligations,
    reviews are anonymized where possible, and access is need-to-work only.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to my report afterward?">
    Free reviews are retained, anonymized, and used to improve the service — that is
    in the authorization you give at upload, in plain words. You can request deletion
    at any time at no cost. If you want guaranteed secure deletion immediately after
    delivery, with a signed certificate for your own vendor-risk records, that is the
    $99 option on the [pricing page](https://soc2doc.ai/pricing).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="How is this different from Vanta, Drata, or Sprinto?">
    Those platforms automate evidence collection — screenshots and config checks.
    They do not read your issued SOC 2, validate that your documentation matches
    reality, sit on auditor calls, or write your policies. soc2doc is the human and
    AI oversight layer that makes those tools work — or replaces them for smaller
    orgs. Full breakdown in [soc2doc vs Vanta, Drata, Sprinto](/why/vs-vanta-drata-sprinto).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I use this alongside my existing compliance platform?">
    Yes — that is the normal setup. Clients keep Vanta or Drata for evidence
    collection and use soc2doc for strategy, policy, and oversight. Complementary by
    design.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Do you perform the audit itself?">
    No, and we never will. SOC 2 audits must be performed by an independent CPA firm.
    We prepare you, help you select the right auditor, and participate in auditor
    calls. The audit report comes from your auditor; independence stays intact.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What if we haven't started SOC 2 at all?">
    Then you do not have a report to upload yet — start with the One Plan, which
    covers templates, weekly Strategy Hours, and the 90-day project plan. Once your
    first report is issued, the Commitment Review is how you keep it honest. See
    [pricing](https://soc2doc.ai/pricing).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Who are the assessors?">
    Security practitioners — CISOs and security leaders who have built and run
    programs themselves — reviewing anonymized extractions matched to their
    expertise. If that is you, [apply to the network](https://soc2doc.ai/assessors).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I cancel anytime?">
    Yes. 30 days written notice, no long-term contracts, no setup fees. The free
    review carries no obligation at all.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
