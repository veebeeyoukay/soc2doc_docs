The compliance-automation platforms — Vanta, Drata, Sprinto — automate evidence
collection: screenshots, config checks, a dashboard, and a to-do list. That is
useful. It is also a vitamin: you operate it, and you get better at carrying the
thing yourself.

soc2doc is the painkiller. Hand it over, and a practitioner takes the whole thing.

## Transfer versus coaching

A platform coaches you: it teaches you to carry SOC 2 better. soc2doc transfers the
load: the work leaves your plate. Both can be true at once —

- **Alongside a platform.** Most clients keep Vanta or Drata for evidence collection
  and use soc2doc for strategy, policy, and oversight — the human and AI checkpoint
  that makes the tooling mean something. Complementary by design.
- **Instead of a platform.** For smaller or earlier-stage teams, soc2doc can replace
  the platform entirely rather than add another dashboard to operate.

## What each one does not do

A platform does not read your issued SOC 2 and tell you whether your documentation
matches reality, does not sit on the auditor calls, and does not write your
policies. A dashboard turning green is not the same as the evidence reflecting
what is actually happening.

soc2doc, for its part, does **not** perform the audit, and the free review does no gap analysis.
We are explicit about both limits so nothing here pretends to be assurance.

<Note>
No competitor is described dishonestly here. The platforms do real, useful work at
evidence collection. The distinction is transfer versus coaching — who ends up
carrying the year.
