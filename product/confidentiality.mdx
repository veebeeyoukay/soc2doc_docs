Three parties see your report, in this order, and nobody else: the automated
extraction pipeline, the vetted assessor who verifies the output before delivery,
and you.

## The order of access

<Steps>
  <Step title="The extraction pipeline">
    An automated pipeline reads the report and extracts commitments, cadences, and
    dependencies. The report is encrypted in transit and at rest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A vetted assessor">
    A practitioner verifies the extracted output before it reaches you. Assessors
    work under written confidentiality obligations, reviews are anonymized where
    possible, and access is need-to-work only.
  </Step>
  <Step title="You">
    The finished Commitment Review arrives by email. It is yours.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## The controls around it

- **Mutual NDA before upload.** The NDA is executed before the upload is even
  enabled. You get a copy by email with the acceptance timestamp. The text is public
  at [soc2doc.ai/nda](https://soc2doc.ai/nda).
- **Separate authorization log.** Your permission for machine and human analysis is
  logged separately, with its own timestamp.
- **Encryption.** The report is encrypted in transit and at rest; stored objects are
  private and are never rendered publicly.
- **Deletion on request.** You can request deletion at any time at no cost. If you
  want guaranteed secure deletion immediately after delivery, with a signed
  certificate for your own vendor-risk records, that is the $99 option on the
  [pricing page](https://soc2doc.ai/pricing).

<Note>
Free reviews are retained and anonymized to improve the service — that is stated
plainly in the authorization you give at upload. Deletion is always available at no
cost, and the $99 option adds a signed certificate.
</Note>
