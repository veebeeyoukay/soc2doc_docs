A Commitment Review is a plain-language mirror of your issued SOC 2 report. You
upload the report; we extract every commitment your organization made in it —
what you promised, how often, and what demonstrating it at audit time will
involve — and hand it back to you organized by business function. No framework
IDs, no control numbers.

It is free, and it arrives the same day.

## What it contains

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Every commitment" icon="list-checks">
    What you promised, in plain language — organized by business function
    (governance, access, vendors, operations). No framework IDs, no control numbers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Every cadence" icon="calendar-clock">
    The stated frequency of each commitment, what demonstrating it at audit time
    will involve, and a "who owns this today?" prompt for each one.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What it deliberately leaves out

The review mirrors what your report says. It does not judge whether your
organization lives up to it, and it does not hand you homework.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="No gap analysis" icon="circle-slash">
    The review does not score you. Judging whether the organization meets its
    commitments requires assessing the organization — interviews, evidence,
    process. That is a paid engagement, not a free document read.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No checklists or templates" icon="circle-slash">
    A checklist would teach you to guess. The review shows you the actual size of
    the year your report committed you to — then you decide who runs it.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
soc2doc never performs the audit. SOC 2 audits are performed by an independent CPA
firm. The Commitment Review reads the report you already have; it does not replace
the auditor, and it does not pretend to be assurance.
</Note>
