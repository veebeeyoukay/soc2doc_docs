title: "The Commitment Review"
description: "What a Commitment Review is: the commitments in your issued SOC 2 mirrored back in plain English, organized for ownership and follow-through."
---

A Commitment Review is a plain-language readout of your issued SOC 2 report. You
upload the report; we extract the commitments your organization made in it - what
was promised, how often, and what demonstrating that commitment usually requires -
and return the results organized by business function.

The free review is meant to make the commitments visible. It is not a scorecard,
and it does not replace the work of assessing the organization.

## What it contains

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Commitments" icon="list-checks">
    The promises stated in the report, rewritten in plain language and grouped by
    business function such as governance, access, vendors, and operations.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Cadence and ownership prompts" icon="calendar-clock">
    The stated frequency of each commitment, what evidence usually has to show, and
    a direct prompt to identify who owns the work today.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## What it deliberately leaves out

The free review mirrors what the report says. It does not judge whether your
organization currently meets those commitments.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="No gap analysis" icon="circle-slash">
    This is not gap analysis. Assessing the organization requires interviews,
    evidence, systems, and process. That is paid advisory work, not a free document
    read.
  </Card>
  <Card title="No generic templates" icon="circle-slash">
    Generic templates can make the work look smaller than it is. The review starts
    with the commitments your own report already made.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
soc2doc never performs the audit. SOC 2 audits are performed by an independent CPA
firm. The Commitment Review reads the report you already have; it does not replace
the auditor and does not provide assurance.
</Note>
